Research Note · Delhi · June 2026

Where to Rent
the Car Out

Research on the platforms and channels for giving our cab to a driver on a daily rent (~₹1,000–1,200/day) — what each one is, who finds the driver, and what it really costs. For reference only; verify live before acting.

01 / 04
What We're Looking For

The Model:
Owner → Driver, Daily Rent

We keep ownership of the car; a vetted driver takes it and pays us a fixed daily rent, covering his own fuel and daily running. We only cover EMI, insurance and maintenance. The rides come via Ola/Uber; the rent is a private arrangement between us and the driver.

✅ This model (what we want)
Driver-operated commercial taxi. Driver pays us ~₹1,000–1,200/day, drives it on apps all day, keeps the fare income, pays fuel. We get predictable rent, near-zero daily effort.
❌ Not this (self-drive)
Zoomcar / Revv / Myles rent the car to a customer who drives it themselves for a trip. Different business, different licence — not our daily-rent-to-a-driver model. Don't confuse the two.

Before We Can Do This

Requirements &
Safeguards

Must-haves (legal)
• Commercial yellow-plate registration
• Contract carriage permit
• Commercial insurance (not private)
• Valid fitness + pollution certificates
• Driver: commercial licence + police verification
(All already in our launch checklist.)
Safeguards (protect the asset)
• Security deposit from the driver
• Written rent agreement (rate, days, responsibilities, damage)
• GPS always on for tracking
• Driver pays fuel, fines & daily running
• Damage from negligence recovered from driver/deposit
• A backup driver contact so income doesn't stop
⚠️ The real challenge isn't the platform — it's the driver. Finding someone reliable who rents for 25+ days every month and treats the car well is the hard part. A renting driver also pushes the car harder (more wear), so factor slightly higher maintenance over time.
