The Rules to
Join & Run This Together

A starting set of fair, clear rules for how 3–6 of us enter the venture, put in money, share profit, run the cab, and make decisions. This is a living document — nothing here is final until we all discuss and agree.

Draft v1 rules we started with Proposed added for fairness — to be voted on
📌 These are working rules to get started and can be changed by group agreement at any time. Once we settle them, they should be written into a proper partnership deed (and ideally checked by a lawyer) so they're legally binding.
Section 1

Membership & Ownership

1
Team size: 3 to 6 peopleDraft v1
The first car is started by a team of 3–6 members, depending on who is genuinely interested and committed.
2
Whose name the car is inDraft v1
The first car can be registered in one member's name (for faster loan/approval). If any member is not comfortable with that, we register it in everyone's name / jointly instead.
3
A common bank accountDraft v1
We open a joint account to collect and spend the venture's money — or a single account that every member can check at any time. All cab income goes here; all expenses come from here.
4
Put it in writingProposed
Once these rules are agreed, sign a simple partnership deed covering shares, profit split, roles and exit. Until then, this charter is our informal understanding.
5
Adding new members laterProposed
A new member can join only with the agreement of all existing members, and on the same share/investment terms decided at that time.
Section 2

Money: Investment & Profit

6
Prefer the zero down-payment optionDraft v1
We try to take a 0 down-payment loan. The group jointly pays the EMI + driver cost + parking (~₹2,000/mo, since we have no space of our own) until the car itself earns enough to cover its own running cost.
7
If zero-down isn't availableDraft v1
We split the required amount among members — equally or share-wise. Profit is then shared in proportion to investment: less money in = smaller profit share (and proportionally more of any loss) 🙂
8
Emergency reserveDraft v1
Always keep at least ₹20,000–30,000 in the common account as a buffer for unfortunate events (repairs, fines, a missed EMI, accident downtime).
9
When profit is shared outProposed
Profit is distributed (monthly or quarterly) only after the EMI, driver, running costs and the emergency reserve are fully covered. During the loan period we may keep/reinvest most profit toward stability or the next car.
10
If extra money is needed (capital call)Proposed
If the car needs more funds than it earns (big repair, EMI shortfall), members top up in proportion to their share. A member who can't or won't contribute has their share reduced accordingly.
Section 3

The 6-Month Test & Exit

11
Review at 6 monthsDraft v1
We run and observe for the first 6 months. If by then the car can cover its running cost → we continue. If not → we jointly discuss, and may drop the plan and sell the car, accepting whatever loss comes.
12
Define "covers its cost" clearlyProposed
To avoid arguments later, agree a number now — e.g. the car earns at least EMI + driver + fuel + maintenance + parking for 2 consecutive months counts as "covering running cost."
13
How we exit if it failsProposed
The decision to sell needs a majority vote. Any loss on sale (and any remaining loan) is shared in proportion to each member's investment.
Section 4

Running the Cab & the Driver

14
Hiring the driverDraft v1
Hire a driver at ₹15,000 + incentive, with 2 or more years of driving experience. Pay on time — a reliable driver is the most important part of this business.
15
Keep all papers validProposed
Commercial insurance, permit, fitness and pollution certificates are always kept up to date, renewed from the common fund. One member is responsible for tracking renewal dates.
16
Fines, accidents & damageProposed
Genuine challans and accident costs are paid from the common fund / insurance. Damage caused by driver negligence is recovered from the driver as per a written understanding with them.
17
Monitoring & a backupProposed
Use the GPS to track the car, and always keep one backup driver contact so a single driver leaving doesn't stop our income.
🅿️ Parking: since none of us has space to park the car, we arrange a monthly night-parking spot (~₹1,500–3,000, budget ₹2,000) paid from the common fund. If any member can offer free/cheaper parking, even better — it directly speeds up our break-even.
Section 5

Personal Use of the Cab

18
Up to 5 days a year, per memberDraft v1
Any member may use the cab for up to 5 days in a year — but it must be told in advance and/or voted on, so it doesn't clash with bookings or another member's plan.
19
Who pays during personal useProposed
The member using the cab covers the fuel and any damage during their days, and avoids peak earning days (festivals, weekends) unless the group agrees. This keeps it fair to everyone.
Section 6

Decisions, Transparency & Disputes

20
How we decide thingsProposed
One member, one vote. Routine decisions need a simple majority; big ones (selling the car, dropping the plan, adding a member, large spends) need a stronger majority — e.g. 75% agreement.
21
Full transparency every monthProposed
A simple monthly income/expense sheet is shared with all members (e.g. in a WhatsApp group + a shared Google Sheet). All earnings go into the common account — nothing off the books.
22
Clear rolesProposed
Split the work so it doesn't fall on one person: a treasurer (accounts), an operations lead (driver & car), and a compliance lead (papers & renewals).
23
Settling disagreementsProposed
Disputes are first discussed openly, then put to a vote. If we're truly deadlocked, we bring in a neutral person we all trust to mediate before things turn sour.
Section 7

Leaving, Inheritance & Changes

24
If a member wants to leaveProposed
Their share is valued fairly (based on the car's worth + cash in the fund, minus loan). Existing members get first right to buy that share before any outsider. Consider a short 6–12 month lock-in at the start so the team stays stable.
25
In case of death or incapacityProposed
Each member names a nominee. If something happens to a member, their share passes to the nominee, or the group buys it out at fair value — agreed kindly and in writing.
26
This is a living documentProposed
Any rule here can be added, removed or changed by agreed vote. Each change is dated and noted, so everyone always knows the current version.
🗓️ Launch Day · A Quick Vibe Check 😄

Before You Sign —
Bring a Clear Mind & a Full Wallet

We launch mid-August, after the August salary lands 💸 — let's not start broke. Before that, sign with a clear head: only join if you can happily either (a) lose this money like a bad IPL bet 🏏, or (b) pretend it doesn't exist for at least ~6 years till the car pays you back. If both stress you out — wait, zero hard feelings. 🙂 Clear mind + full wallet, then we ride. 🚕✨
