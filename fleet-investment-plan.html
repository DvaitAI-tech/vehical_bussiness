Where We Park & Grow Money

Investment Options
Compared

Before buying cars, the partners' monthly contributions sit and grow somewhere. The instrument decides how fast the corpus reaches each car's down payment. Realistic options for India in 2026.

Recommended Mix
Equity SIP (~12%) as the growth engine — a long horizon smooths out market dips. FD/RD (~7%) only for the emergency buffer that must stay safe. Money needed within 12 months sits in a debt fund, never equity.
Why Not Just FD?
At ~7%, FD barely beats inflation (~6%). Over 10 years, ₹5,000/mo in FD grows to ~₹8.6L, but in a 12% SIP it becomes ~₹11.6L per partner — roughly ₹3L more, simply from the instrument. That gap funds an extra car.

10-Car Verdict — After 5 Years of Running

Profit — But Slower

Each partner invests ~₹3L and, across Yr 6–10, receives ~₹4.8L in distributions — plus a ⅕ share of a fleet still worth ~₹20–30L (≈ ₹4–6L each). Call it a ~3× return over the decade. The big money lands late (Yr 9–10) because most loans run till then — and the fleet carries ~₹40L of debt at its peak. Parking and honest loan-timing make this clearly less rosy than it first looks, so read the reality check before celebrating.

The Honest Part

Reality Check &
Real Risks

A polished plan is easy; surviving years of fleet operations is hard. These decide whether the profit is real or fantasy.

The Uncomfortable Comparison: Fleet vs Just-SIP-the-Money
If a partner instead put the same ~₹3L (₹5,000/mo for 5 yrs) into a plain 12% SIP and left it 10 years, it grows to ~₹7.5L — with zero effort, zero loans, zero risk of ruin.
The fleet returns ~₹4.8L cash + asset ≈ ₹9–11L — only about 1.3–1.5× the lazy SIP, once parking and real loan-timing are honest. Thin upside for a decade of managing drivers, debt and risk. Slip 20% on operations and the fleet loses to the SIP it was meant to beat — which is exactly why we build to 5 first, not 10.
👷
Drivers Are the Real Ceiling
More cars = more drivers to recruit, retain and police. Churn and theft — not money — is what sinks fleets. Scale only as fast as you can manage drivers.
📉
Margins Are Utilization-Sensitive
The model assumes ~₹80K gross/car. At 70% of that, per-car net can halve. A few idle cars erase a month's profit and push break-even later.
🏦
Leverage Cuts Both Ways
A 10-car fleet carries ~₹40L debt at peak. A rate hike or a bad quarter with many EMIs due is dangerous. Keep a 3-month EMI buffer always.
🔁
The Replacement Treadmill
Cars wear out in ~7 years of taxi use. At scale you're permanently buying replacements — capex that never stops and caps long-run profit.
⚡
Regulatory / EV Shock
If Delhi mandates EV fleets mid-decade, swapping CNG cars for EVs is a large unplanned capex. Watch policy closely.
🧑‍💼
It Becomes a Full Job
Beyond 4–5 cars this isn't passive income — someone runs it daily, or you pay a manager (~₹30K/mo, already in the numbers).
Honest recommendation: Build to 5 cars first — you break even by ~Year 7 with manageable debt and drivers. Only push toward 10 if, by Year 3, drivers and utilization are genuinely under control. Keep a parallel SIP running as the benchmark every new car must beat.

Assumptions
Behind the Numbers

Every figure here is a realistic planning estimate, not a guarantee. The model rests on these — change any one and the break-even date moves.

• 5 partners, equal split
• Car: WagonR Tour CNG, ~₹7.5L on-road
• ₹2.2L launch cash/car · ₹6L loan @ ~9% · 5-yr EMI ₹12,450
• One car added per year (5-car path)
• Net per car (after parking): ~₹3K/mo under loan → ~₹14K/mo after loan
• Gross ~₹80K/car/mo (realistic, not best-case)
• Parking / night stand rent ~₹2,000/car/mo
• EMIs & running costs self-paid by each car
• Equity SIP ~12% · FD/RD ~7% returns assumed
• Car taxi life ~7 yrs before replacement
• No loan prepayment; no major shocks priced in
⚠️ Verify before committing: live car prices, current bank rates, the prevailing Delhi EV/CNG policy, and real local earnings from drivers already running cabs. Treat Year 1 with one car as the true test of every assumption above.
